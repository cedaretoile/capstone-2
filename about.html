Cedar Étoile

Cedar Étoile is an Indigenous photographer, storyteller, and steward based on so-called Vancouver Island, British Columbia. They take pride in being Kwakwaka'wakw, Nuu-chah-nulth, and Métis. Cedar was raised with Indigenous values; language, culture, and traditions. They hold these values closely and respectfully shares them through modern technology.

In recent years, Cedar has been involved in tourism and recreation, particularly with advertisement and marketing. It started with exploring the Alberni Valley and taking photos on their own. Then worked with the City of Port Alberni, on their previous social media project, The Heart of Vancouver Island as content creator and events manager - coordinator. Shortly after, Cedar worked at the Port Hardy Visitor Centre as Lead Visitor Counsellor, Social Media & Marketing Manager. It was during this time Cedar created the well received 2018 Port Hardy Tourism advertising campaign. Nowadays, Cedar is a Ditidaht Guardian on the West Coast Trail and freelance photographer & writer. Cedar graduated from Vancouver Island University with a certificate in Adventure and Recreation Tourism.
